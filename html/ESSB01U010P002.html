Unit 10   Exercise 2
Write am/is/are (present) or was/were (past).
1   Last year she  was  22, so she  is  23 now.
2   Today the weather    nice, but yesterday it    very cold.
Today the weather is nice, but yesterday it was very cold.
3   I    hungry.  Can I have something to eat?
I’m / am  hungry.  Can I have something to eat?
4   I feel fine this morning, but I    very tired last night.
I feel fine this morning, but I was very tired last night.
5   Where    you at 11 o’clock last Friday morning?
Where were you at 11 o’clock last Friday morning?
6   Don’t buy those shoes.  They    very expensive.
Don’t buy those shoes.  They’re / are  very expensive.
7   I like your new jacket.    it expensive?
I like your new jacket.  Was it expensive?
8   This time last year I    in Paris.
This time last year I was in Paris.
9   ‘Where    Sam and Joe?’     ‘I don’t know.  They    here a few
minutes ago.’
‘Where are Sam and Joe?’     ‘I don’t know.  They were here a few
minutes ago.’
Unit 10   Exercise 3
Write was/were or wasn’t/weren’t.
1    We weren’t happy with the hotel.  Our room  was  very small
and it  wasn’t  clean.
2    Mark    at work last week because he    ill.  He’s better now.
Mark wasn’t at work last week because he was ill.  He’s better now.
3    Yesterday    a public holiday, so the banks    closed.
They’re open today.
Yesterday was a public holiday, so the banks were closed.  They’re open today.
4    ‘   Kate and Ben at the party?’     ‘Kate    there, but
Ben    .’
‘Were Kate and Ben at the party?’     ‘Kate was there, but Ben wasn’t.’   or
‘Kate wasn’t there, but Ben was.’
5    Where are my keys?  They    on the table, but they’re not there now.
Where are my keys?  They were on the table, but they’re not there now.
6    You    at home last night.  Where    you?
You weren’t at home last night.  Where were you?
