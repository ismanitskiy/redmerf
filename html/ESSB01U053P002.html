Unit 53   Exercise 3
Complete these sentences with the verbs in the list.  Sometimes to is necessary (to go /
to wait etc.); sometimes to is not necessary (go/wait etc.).
arrive      borrow      get        go       go        make      repeat      tell      think      wait
1     Please stay here.  I don’t want you  to go  yet.
2     I didn’t hear what she said, so I asked her    it.
I didn’t hear what she said, so I asked her to repeat it.
3     ‘Shall we begin?’    ‘No, let’s    a few minutes.’
‘Shall we begin?’    ‘No, let’s wait a few minutes.’
4     Are they already here?  I expected them    much later.
Are they already here?  I expected them to arrive much later.
5     Kevin’s parents didn’t want him    married.
Kevin’s parents didn’t want him to get married.
6     I want to stay here.  You can’t make me    with you.
I want to stay here.  You can’t make me go with you.
7     ‘Is that your bike?’   ‘No, it’s John’s.  He let me    it.’
‘Is that your bike?’   ‘No, it’s John’s.  He let me borrow it.’
8     Rachel can’t come to the party.  She told me    you.
Rachel can’t come to the party.  She told me to tell you.
9     Would you like a drink?  Would you like me    some coffee?
Would you like a drink?  Would you like me to make (or to get) some coffee?
10     ‘Kate doesn’t like me.’    ‘What makes you    that?’
‘Kate doesn’t like me.’      ‘What makes you think that?’
