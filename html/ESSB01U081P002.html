Unit 81   Exercise 1
Complete the sentences.  Use the word in brackets (some/most etc.).
Sometimes you need of (some of / most of etc.).
1   Most  children like playing.   (most)
2   Some of  this money is yours.   (some)
3     people never stop talking.   (some)
Some people never stop talking.
4     the shops in the city centre close at 6.30.   (most)
Most of the shops in the city centre close at 6.30.
5     people have mobile phones these days.   (most)
Most people have mobile phones these days.
6   I don’t like    the pictures in the living room.   (any)
I don’t like any of the pictures in the living room.
7   He’s lost    his money.   (all)
He’s lost  all / all of  his money.
8     my friends are married.   (none)
None of my friends are married.
9   Do you know    the people in this picture?   (any)
Do you know any of the people in this picture?
10     birds can fly.   (most)
Most birds can fly.
11   I enjoyed    the film, but I didn’t like the ending.   (most)
I enjoyed most of the film, but I didn’t like the ending.
12     sports are very dangerous.   (some)
Some sports are very dangerous.
13   We can’t find anywhere to stay.    the hotels are full.   (all)
We can’t find anywhere to stay.  All / All of  the hotels are full.
14   You must have    this cheese.  It’s delicious.   (some)
You must have some of this cheese.  It’s delicious.
15   The weather was bad when we were on holiday.  It rained    the time.  
(most)
The weather was bad when we were on holiday.  It rained most of the time.
