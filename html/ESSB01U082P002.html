Unit 82   Exercise 1
Write both/either/neither.  Use of where necessary.
1   Last year I went to Paris and Rome.  I liked  both  cities very much.
2   There were two pictures on the wall.  I didn’t like  either of  them.
3   It was a good football match.    teams played well.
It was a good football match.   Both teams played well.
4   It wasn’t a good football match.    team played well.
It wasn’t a good football match.   Neither team played well.
5   ‘Is your friend English or American?’     ‘    .  She’s Australian.’
‘Is your friend English or American?’     ‘Neither.  She’s Australian.’
6   We went away for two days, but the weather wasn’t good.  It rained  
days.
We went away for two days, but the weather wasn’t good.  It rained both days.
7   A:  I bought two newspapers.  Which one do you want?
B:    .  It doesn’t matter which one.
B:  Either.  It doesn’t matter which one.
8   I invited Sam and Chris to the party, but    them came.
I invited Sam and Chris to the party, but neither of them came.
9   ‘Do you go to work by car or by bus?’     ‘    .  I always walk.’
‘Do you go to work by car or by bus?’     ‘Neither.  I always walk.’
10   ‘Which jacket do you prefer, this one or that one?’     ‘I don’t like    them.’
‘Which jacket do you prefer, this one or that one?’     ‘I don’t like either of them.’
11   ‘Do you work or are you a student?’     ‘    .  I work and I‘m a student too.’
‘Do you work or are you a student?’     ‘Both.  I work and I’m a student too.’
12   My friend and I went to the cinema, but    us liked the film.  It was
really bad.
My friend and I went to the cinema, but neither of us liked the film.  It was really bad.
13   Helen has two sisters and a brother.    sisters are married.
Helen has two sisters and a brother.  Both sisters are married.
14   Helen has two sisters and a brother.  I’ve met her brother, but I haven‘t met
  her sisters.
Helen has two sisters and a brother.  I’ve met her brother, but I haven’t met either of her sisters.
