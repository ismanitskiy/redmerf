Unit 100   Exercise 1
Complete the sentences.
1   I don’t know the answer.  If I  knew  the answer, I’d tell you.
2   I have a car.  I couldn’t travel very much if I  didn’t have  a car.
3   I don’t want to go out.  If I    to go out, I’d go.
I don’t want to go out.  If I wanted to go out, I’d go.
4   We don’t have a key.  If we    a key, we could get into the house.
We don’t have a key.  If we had a key, we could get into the house.
5   I’m not hungry.  I would have something to eat if I    hungry.
I’m not hungry. I would have something to eat if I  were / was  hungry.
6   Sue enjoys her work.  She wouldn’t do it if she    it.
Sue enjoys her work.  She wouldn’t do it if she didn’t enjoy it.
7   He can’t speak any foreign languages.  If he    speak a foreign
language, perhaps he would get a better job.
He can’t speak any foreign languages.  If he could speak a foreign language,
perhaps he would get a better job.
8   You don’t try hard enough.  If you    harder, you would have more
success.
You don’t try hard enough.  If you tried harder, you would have more
success.
9   I have a lot to do today.  If I    so much to do, we could go out.
I have a lot to do today.  If I didn’t have so much to do, we could go out.
Unit 100   Exercise 2
Put the verb in the correct form.
1   If  he had  the money, he would buy a fast car.
(he/have)
2   Jane likes living in a city.  She wouldn’t be  happy if she lived in the country.
(she/not/be)
3   If I wanted to learn Italian,    to Italy.
(I go)
If I wanted to learn Italian,  I’d go / I would go  to Italy.
4   I haven’t told Helen what happened.  She’d be angry if    .
(she/know)
I haven’t told Helen what happened.  She’d be angry if she knew.
5   If    a map, I could show you where I live.
(we/have)
If we had a map, I could show you where I live.
6   What would you do if    a lot of money?
(you/win)
What would you do if you won a lot of money?
7   It’s not a very good hotel.    there if I were you.
(I/not/stay)
It’s not a very good hotel.  I wouldn’t stay there if I were you.
8   If    nearer London, we would go there more often.
(we/live)
If we lived nearer London, we would go there more often.
9   It’s a shame you have to go now.    nice if you had more time.
(it/be)
It’s a shame you have to go now.  It would be nice if you had more time.
10   I’m not going to take the job.  I’d take it if   better.
(the salary/be)
I’m not going to take the job.  I’d take it if the salary  was / were  better.
11   I don’t know anything about cars.  If the car broke down,  
what to do. (I/not/know)
I don’t know anything about cars.  If the car broke down, I wouldn’t know what
to do.
12   If you could change one thing in the world, what    ?
(you/change)
If you could change one thing in the world, what would you change?
Unit 100   Exercise 3
Complete the sentences.  Choose from the box and put the verb in the correct form.
we (have) a bigger house                           it (be) a bit cheaper 
we (buy) a bigger house                             the air (be) cleaner
we (have) some pictures on the wall      I (watch) it
every day (be) the same                             I (be) bored
1   I’d buy that jacket if  it was a bit cheaper  .
2   If there was a good film on TV tonight,    .
If there was a good film on TV tonight,  I’d watch it / I would watch it.
3   This room would be nicer if    .
his room would be nicer if we had some pictures on the wall.
4   If there wasn’t so much traffic,    .
If there wasn’t so much traffic, the air would be cleaner.
5   Life would be boring if    .
Life would be boring if every day  was / were  the same.
6   If I had nothing to do,    .
If I had nothing to do,  I’d be bored / I would be bored.
7   We could invite all our friends to stay if    .
We could invite all our friends to stay if  we had a bigger house / we
bought a bigger house.
8   If we had more money,    .
If we had more money, we  would / could  buy a bigger house  or  
we  would / could  have a bigger house.
