Unit 113   Exercise 2
Complete the sentences with to/for/about etc.  Two sentences are already complete,
and no word is necessary.
  1   Thank you very much  for  your help.
  2   This isn’t my umbrella.  It belongs    a friend of mine.
This isn’t my umbrella.  It belongs to a friend of mine.
  3   I saw Steve, but I didn’t speak    him.
I saw Steve, but I didn’t speak to him.
  4   Don’t forget to phone    your mother tonight.
Don’t forget to phone your mother tonight.
  5   Thank you    the present.  It was lovely.
Thank you for the present.  It was lovely.
  6   What happened    Ella last night?  Why didn’t she come to the party?
What happened to Ella last night?  Why didn’t she come to the party?
  7   We’re thinking    going to Australia next year.
We’re thinking  of  /  about  going to Australia next year.
  8   We asked the waiter    coffee, but he brought us tea.
We asked the waiter for coffee, but he brought us tea.
  9   ‘Do you like reading books?’    ‘It depends    the book.’
‘Do you like reading books?’    ‘It depends on the book.’
10   John was talking, but nobody was listening    what he was saying.
John was talking, but nobody was listening to what he was saying.
11   We waited    Karen until 2 o’clock, but she didn’t come.
We waited for Karen until 2 o’clock, but she didn’t come.
12   I texted    Lisa to tell her I would be late.
I texted Lisa to tell her I would be late.
13   He’s alone all day.  He never talks    anybody.
He’s alone all day.  He never talks to anybody.
14   ‘How much does it cost to stay at this hotel?’   ‘It depends    the type of
room.’
‘How much does it cost to stay at this hotel?’   ‘It depends on the type of room.’
15   Catherine is thinking    changing her job.
Catherine is thinking  of  /  about  changing her job.
